# 02 VX Philosophy Page Design

Date: 2026-07-22
Status: Approved and implemented
Reference: NorthSand Philosophy information hierarchy; 02 Paper Circuit visual system

## 1. Objective

Create a standalone `/philosophy/` page that explains what 02 believes, why VX exists, how the belief changes daily behavior, and how it appears in client work.

The page must work for two audiences at once:

- Clients and partners should understand why 02 can make transformation take root after DX or AI introduction.
- Candidates and team members should understand how people at 02 are expected to think and act.

The page is not a collection of inspirational slogans. It moves from problem recognition to mission, behavior, operating method, and the founder's personal principle.

## 2. Fixed Brand Hierarchy

The hierarchy is fixed as follows:

1. **Core Value** — the fundamental view of transformation.
2. **Mission** — the change 02 promises to create.
3. **Values** — five behavioral standards used in daily decisions.
4. **How We Work** — the existing 0→1→2 method that converts the philosophy into delivery.
5. **Founder's Principle** — a personal source of conviction, intentionally separated from the corporate Mission and Values.

This preserves the depth of the NorthSand pattern without copying its words, rules, illustrations, or layout.

## 4. Page Architecture

The approved A-02 structure is a long-form editorial page.

### 4.1 Header

- Reuse the 02 sticky header and Paper Circuit styling.
- The brand links to `/`.
- Philosophy is visually marked as the current page.
- Services, Works, About, and Contact link back to their homepage anchors.
- Mobile navigation preserves keyboard, Escape, and scroll-lock behavior.

### 4.2 Hero — Core Value

- Large `VIBES TRANSFORMATION` typography.
- Oversized outlined `V` and coral `X` act as the primary visual asset.
- Lead statement: `変革は、熱で完成する。`
- Short explanation introduces the distinction between technology installation and human transformation.
- Paper Circuit lines remain lower contrast than the text.

### 4.3 Why VX

- Two-column editorial section.
- Left: `変革は、導入では終わらない。`
- Right: the approved VX body copy.
- The section explains the missing piece before introducing frameworks.

### 4.4 DX → AX → VX

- Three connected stages.
- DX: `道具を変える。仕事の土台をデジタルへ。`
- AX: `仕事を変える。人とAIの役割を組み直す。`
- VX: `人を変える。変革を自分たちの意志にする。`
- VX receives the strongest blue/coral emphasis.

### 4.5 Mission

- Full-width black field.
- Mission is the dominant type.
- `やりたくなる変革` is highlighted in coral.
- Supporting copy remains secondary and readable.

### 4.6 Values

- Five full-width editorial rows rather than small cards.
- Every row contains number, title, and explanation.
- Alternating alignment or subtle circuit changes create rhythm without changing the information hierarchy.
- Pointer interaction may reveal a heat trace; content never depends on hover.

### 4.7 How We Work — 0→1→2

- Connect the philosophy to the existing company method.
- 0 / Discover: enter the field and define the real question.
- 1 / Build: make a small working form and return learning to implementation.
- 2 / Grow: turn it into a system, root it in operations, and grow results.
- Desktop uses a horizontal circuit; mobile uses a vertical circuit.

### 4.8 Founder's Principle

- Large cobalt field near the end of the page.
- Reveal the three lines as three deliberate beats.
- A restrained door-outline motion appears only on the final line.
- Attribution remains visible but secondary.
- Avoid crosses, stained glass, or other religious visual symbols. The section is personal conviction, not religious branding.

### 4.9 CTA

- Coral closing field.
- Heading: `次の変革に、火をつけよう。`
- CTA: `相談する` / `START A CONVERSATION →`
- Link to `/#contact`.

## 6. Motion Direction — Heat Circuit Motion

Motion must explain transformation, not decorate empty space.

### 6.1 Hero Heat Field

- Extend the current Paper Circuit language with a dedicated lightweight WebGL canvas.
- Fine lines, connection points, and paper-grain pressure remain non-neon.
- A coral heat pulse travels through the circuit.
- The outlined `V` drifts subtly; the `X` fills from left to right as the page settles.
- Pointer movement attracts nearby circuit nodes on pointer-capable devices only.

### 6.2 Evolution Pulse

- An IntersectionObserver starts a single DX→AX→VX traversal.
- The pulse pauses briefly at each stage.
- VX fills last and remains active.
- Re-entry does not repeatedly restart the full sequence unless the page is reloaded.

### 6.3 Mission Copy Transform

- `やらされる変革` appears first in muted type.
- It recedes or receives a restrained strike-through.
- `やりたくなる変革` enters with a coral underline or wipe.
- Both phrases remain present in the DOM and readable without animation.

### 6.4 Values Cascade

- Rows reveal in order with 80–120ms stagger.
- A circuit trace connects one row to the next.
- Hover/focus moves a coral heat band through the active row.
- Keyboard focus receives the same visible state as hover.

### 6.5 Journey Draw

- The 0→1→2 connector draws as the section enters.
- Nodes activate in sequence.
- Mobile swaps to a vertical path without changing reading order.

### 6.6 Three Knocks

- The founder quote reveals one line at a time.
- The last line triggers a minimal door-outline opening motion.
- Do not use sound, vibration, or full-screen flashes.

### 6.7 CTA Response

- The coral field subtly changes paper pressure with pointer position.
- CTA arrow advances on hover and focus.
- The interaction remains functional with JavaScript disabled.

## 7. Technical Structure

Create isolated page assets so the existing homepage stays stable:

- `philosophy/index.html` — standalone semantic page.
- `assets/css/philosophy.css` — Philosophy-only layout and motion styling.
- `assets/js/philosophy-motion.js` — reveal sequencing, pointer reactions, lifecycle control.
- `assets/js/vx-field.js` — Philosophy WebGL heat field with static fallback.

Modify shared files only where required:

- `index.html` — navigation links and teaser CTA.
- `assets/css/style.css` — shared navigation/current-page state only.
- `sitemap.xml` — add `https://zerotwo.tokyo/philosophy/`.
- `README.md` — architecture, motion budgets, verification, and understanding checklist.

No framework or third-party animation dependency is introduced. The page remains GitHub Pages-compatible static HTML, CSS, and JavaScript.

## 8. Fallbacks and Edge Cases

- HTML contains all content in final reading order before JavaScript runs.
- If WebGL initialization fails, hide the canvas and retain the paper-grid background, V/X typography, and all content.
- If IntersectionObserver is unavailable, show every section in its completed state.
- If JavaScript is disabled, navigation, content, quote, and CTA remain usable.
- `prefers-reduced-motion: reduce` disables continuous movement, stagger, pointer attraction, and door animation; show the completed visual state.
- Pause WebGL while its canvas is outside the viewport or the tab is hidden.
- Resume without time jumps or burst animation.
- Do not read cookies, local storage, or user-identifying data for motion.

## 9. Performance Budget

- Desktop WebGL target: maximum 24fps, device-pixel-ratio capped at 1.25.
- Mobile WebGL target: maximum 18fps, render density capped at 0.9.
- Pause off-screen and on `document.hidden`.
- Avoid layout-triggering animation; use transform, opacity, background position, and shader time uniforms.
- Keep new uncompressed JavaScript under approximately 30KB total.
- Lighthouse mobile target: Performance 95+, Accessibility 100, Best Practices 100, SEO 100.
- CLS target: 0.

## 10. Responsive and Accessibility Requirements

- Validate at 320, 360, 375, 390, 680, 681, 767, 768, 980, 981, 1024, and 1440px.
- No horizontal overflow or overlap at any checked width.
- Hero statement remains legible without single-character orphan lines.
- DX→AX→VX becomes a vertical sequence below 681px.
- Values retain number → title → explanation order on mobile.
- The quote remains three intentional lines where space allows and may wrap naturally within each line on 320px screens.
- Use one `h1`, followed by sequential `h2` and `h3` levels.
- Decorative V/X and canvas are `aria-hidden`.
- Navigation, Values interactions, and CTA are fully keyboard reachable.
- Color contrast meets WCAG AA.
- Browser console has zero errors and warnings on the homepage, Philosophy, Privacy, and 404 pages.

## 13. Understanding Checklist

Before handoff, the site owner should be able to explain:

- Why VX is a Core Value and not a sixth behavioral Value.
- Why Mission describes the change promised to clients and employees.
- Why the five Values are written as observable actions.
- Why 0→1→2 belongs after Values as the delivery mechanism.
- Why the founder quote is separated from corporate Mission and Values.
- Why every animation represents heat transfer, activation, or adoption.
- Why WebGL pauses off-screen and why reduced-motion shows a static completed state.
- How the homepage teaser and dedicated Philosophy page divide information.
